The solar corona is the place where the solar wind is heated and accelerated, there are impulsive events as solar flares and CMEs, and also the ratio of the abundances freeze out in the solar corona that's why observation and modeling of the solar corona is important to have a better understanding of the sun-earth environment.


In these direction we are trying to validate part of the space weather modeling framework, which is a 3D mhd model.  

3) The plasma in the solar corona can be found in a width range of density and temperatures. over the chromosphere (the black line) the density decay but the temperature increase due to the coronal heating.
The corona is optically thin in the extreme ultraviolet range and in white light. We are using both to obtain physical information of the plasma.

We observe the sun through images which of course are 2D projections of 3D emitting structures.
Advancement of physical models are in need of 3D information of the coronal fundamental parameters like B, N, T and chemical abundances.

To that end we are using a solar rotational tomographic technique.

Cecilia told us about the differential emission measure tomographic technique using euv images but with a few changes we have applied a tomographic technique using withe light images. 


4) The main idea is take images of an entire solar rotation which provides all the lines of sight. 

There are several components  in the coronal light, depending on the physical mechanism.
One of them is the Corona E which is emission by Ferrum ions, emitting in the EUV range. The results of applying the technique to these images are 3D band emissivity and applying the differencial emission measure technique provides a proxy of the density and electron temperature. These emission is in the range of 1.02 and 1.3 solar radii.

We can observer the K- corona which is in a range from 1.1 to several solar radii. we can observe these range due to the thomson scattering of photospheric white light using coronographs. And the technique results are in these case the density.
